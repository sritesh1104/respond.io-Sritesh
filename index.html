Written as a PM would scope this before a single line of code — problem, goal, metrics, approach, scope.

Problem

CX and growth teams often only find out an account is unhappy after it churns or contracts. The signals are already sitting in support conversations, product usage, and account activity — they're just scattered, unscored, and invisible until someone manually digs in during a churn post-mortem. That's reactive, not proactive, and it's exactly the gap a CX Program Manager role is asked to close when digging into why revenue from existing customers grows, shrinks, or disappears.

Goal

Give CX and growth teams a single, explainable view of which accounts are trending toward churn or contraction — early enough to actually intervene — by having AI synthesize sentiment, usage, and engagement signals into one risk score with a plain-language reason attached.

Success metrics

  • Number of high-risk accounts contacted within 48 hours of being flagged
  • Reduction in "surprise" churn — accounts that cancel without ever being flagged
  • Retained-revenue uplift attributable to proactive saves vs. reactive win-backs

Note: "NRR at risk" here is a snapshot proxy, not the textbook calculation — it's the share of current portfolio revenue sitting in flagged accounts, not revenue measured against a prior-period baseline. A production version would need a trailing period of actual billing data to compute true NRR. Only accounts flagged Medium or High risk count toward this figure — Low-risk accounts are excluded.

How it works

This prototype (v1): a transparent, tunable heuristic model — sentiment trend in support conversations, usage decline, response latency, and negative-intent keywords — combined into a weighted score with the reasoning shown per account. Explainability is the point: a CS rep won't act on a black-box number, but they will act on "usage dropped 42% and they mentioned a competitor twice."

Production path (v2): replace the heuristics with an LLM classifier prompted over full conversation history and usage telemetry, and add a feedback loop where reps mark flags accurate or not so the scoring recalibrates over time instead of running on fixed weights indefinitely.

Scope

Scope is deliberately narrow for v1 — the goal is to prove the scoring logic and the explainability pattern work before investing in the harder infrastructure around them.

In scope (v1) — built and demoable now:

  • Risk scoring engine across four signals, with tunable weights
  • Explainable, plain-language reasoning attached to every flagged account
  • Suggested next-best-action per risk tier
  • Aggregate KPI tracking — accounts monitored, NRR at risk, reviews completed

Out of scope (v1) — deferred on purpose, not forgotten:

  • Live data pipeline — the scoring logic needs to be proven on data we can fully control and inspect before it's trusted anywhere near real customer information
  • Automated outreach — the system flags and suggests, a human still decides and acts, so customers don't get a robotic "we noticed you might churn" message
  • Continuous model retraining — there isn't enough real outcome data yet, so weight updates stay human-reviewed for now
